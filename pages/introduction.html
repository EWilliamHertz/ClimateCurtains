The Problem with Windows

Windows are essential for light and aesthetics but are often the weakest link in energy efficiency, with insulation values just a tenth of walls. The problem with heating buildings is that a tremendous amount of heat disappears through the windows.

With the insulation value often to just a tenth of those of walls, windows are essentially holes in walls that leak energy. This leads to significant heat loss, higher energy bills, and increased carbon footprint.

In an average house, 30-50% of heating costs are due to heat loss through windows, which amounts to approximately a loss of 5000 kWh a year. In public and commercial buildings such as schools, offices, and other industrial buildings, the percentage of heat loss through windows is even greater, especially if there is a lot of glass in the structure.

Most Buildings Stand Empty

While windows account for about a third of the energy loss for heating, offices and school buildings are not in use 60-75% of the time. This means that if the windows in a vacant building are covered with highly-insulating curtains, a lot of energy and money will be saved.

This presents a significant opportunity for energy conservation. By implementing ClimateCurtains in buildings during periods of vacancy, we can dramatically reduce energy waste and costs.

Our solution is particularly effective for commercial and public buildings with large window areas and predictable periods of non-use, such as nights, weekends, and holidays.

A New Generation of Climate-Smart Curtains

ClimateCurtains is the solution. Our patented product retains the heat energy inside the building effectively. Climate Curtains is a Swedish company based in Vänersborg, dedicated to sustainable innovation.

Our patented roller blinds are designed to reduce heat loss, provide light control, and suit any building—from modern homes to heritage-listed properties. Save energy, lower costs, and embrace sustainability without compromising style.

Independent testing in collaboration with Chalmers University and supported by the Swedish Energy Agency has proven that our curtains can save at least 15% of heating energy in a typical Swedish family home, with even greater savings in harsher climates or buildings with larger, less-insulated windows.
